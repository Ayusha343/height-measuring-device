- Aim of the project: -
To measure the height of a person accurately without
any hassle.
- Theory behind the project: -
It uses high frequency sound waves (ultrasonic waves)
to resonate a desired frequency and convert electric
energy into acoustic energy, and vice versa. Sound
waves are transmitted to and reflected from the target
back to the transducer.

## • Working: -
The Ultra Sonic Sensor transmits high frequency sound
waves. These sound waves when are reflected of a
person’s head it measures the distance between it and
person’s head by simply multiplying speed of sound, i.e.
around 343m/s, by time taken. Then it finds out the
height of the person by subtracting the distance
between it and person’s head from the height it is
placed, which is 200 cm in this scenario.
## • Advantages: -
(i) It is more accurate than most of the traditional
methods. (Under special circumstances)
(ii) It is less time consuming and hassle free.
(iii) It can be accurate up to 8 decimal places. (Under special circumstances)
(iv) It can save time in mass health check-ups and
measurements.
(v) Can be used by elderly person easily.
## • Applications: -
(i) Can be used in Medical Centres and Hospitals to save
time during community check-ups.
(ii) Can be used in old age homes and orphanages for
convenience.
(iii) When calibrated, it can be used to measure small
objects.
